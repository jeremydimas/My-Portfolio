Description

I did research with a partner and a graduate student involving Machine Learning and artifcial intelligence to train a neural network to analyze 3245 images and identify fiber optic cables (red highlight) and waveguides (green highlight) using a software program called "Computer Vision Annotation Tool: Artificial Intelligence (CVAT.AI)" and Python using Google Colab to peform a 70%-30% split of training and testing data to optimize the performance of an accelerometer.

Objective

I worked with a partner under the supervision of Dr. Chee Wei Wong and Graduate mentor, Connor Nasseraddin, to annotate 3245 images of fiber optic cables and waveguides using CVAT.AI to encode the images in Python using Google Colab to validate the data analysis and precisionment. The validated data will be used to improve an accelerometer's precisionment and accuracy identifying fiber optic cables and waveguides. At the end of the quarter, a presentation will be given on the understanding of the research and future expectations.

Skills

Throughout the course of my research, I learned to be responsible with my time, work effectively and efficiently with my team by attending info-progress meetings, and improve my public-speaking and presentation skills by speaking to over 80 engineering undergrads and 20 graduate students.

Process

Our team met twice weekly over the span of 9 weeks with the first 4 weeks covering the context of our involvement in relation to the accelerometer and its complexities, while the last 5 weeks consisted of my partner and I annotating the 3245 images and creating a presentation of our research to present on the 10th week of the quarter, following the end of our 9-week research.
