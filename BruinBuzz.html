BruinBuzz

Objective

In the Spring Quarter of 2024, I enrolled in the "Turn an Idea into a Company" class at UCLA. The course's objective was to transform an idea into a viable company within nine weeks. Noticing a gap in the availability of a centralized platform for students to discover clubs, organizations, and workshops on campus, I co-founded BruinBuzz, an iOS app designed to streamline access to this information.

My Role

As the CEO and lead developer for BruinBuzz, I guided the project from conception to launch, leveraging Swift programming to build the app's core functionality. I structured team meetings, set clear deadlines, and managed both the technical and business aspects, ensuring the project stayed on track. My focus on enhancing the user experience led to a refined app UI, while effective marketing strategies boosted campus visibility. Collaborating with my team sharpened my problem-solving skills and perseverance through challenges. Presenting the app to investors not only refined my pitching abilities but also built my confidence and public speaking skills.

Process


UCLA lacked an everyday online platform where students could easily discover the whereabouts of various campus activities. The current solution, Bruinwalk, involved physical promoters who were often ignored by students rushing to classes. Recognizing this gap, I initiated the development of BruinBuzz, an app to centralize information about club events, workshops, and promotional activities, making it accessible at students' fingertips.

Throughout the development process, we faced numerous challenges. Learning Swift from scratch was demanding, but it was essential for creating a functional iOS app. We structured our work by setting deadlines and organizing meetings to track progress. Collaborating closely, we continuously improved the app's UI, focusing on optimizing performance and considering the user's perspective. Despite our efforts, we faced two rejections from Apple before our app was finally approved on May 21st, 2024.

Following the app's approval, we successfully launched BruinBuzz on the App Store. On May 30th, our team presented BruinBuzz during our class's "Demo Day" to a group of investors, including Michael Stoppleman, brother of Yelp's Co-Founder and CEO, Jeremy Stoppleman. This presentation was a significant milestone, providing us with valuable feedback and boosting our confidence. The development and launch of BruinBuzz were invaluable experiences that taught me essential skills in programming, business management, and leadership. The process of turning an idea into a functional app within nine weeks was both challenging and rewarding.

Future Direction

I acquired and honed several skills. I quickly adapted to programming in Swift, learned effective business management techniques, and embraced the responsibilities of being a CEO. Leading a team taught me the importance of collaboration and organization. I improved my problem-solving abilities by addressing challenges like app rejections and continuously enhancing the app. The marketing efforts helped me develop effective promotion strategies and refine my pitching skills. Presenting to investors further enhanced my confidence and public speaking abilities. I am proud of my team's accomplishments and look forward to continuing to improve and promote BruinBuzz. This experience has been the highlight of my time at UCLA, providing me with practical knowledge and a strong foundation for future endeavors.
