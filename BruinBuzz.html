BruinBuzz

Objective

Transform a viable idea into a fully operational company by identifying a market opportunity and launching the business within 9 weeks.

Skills

Developing and launching BruinBuzz taught me valuable skills in Swift programming, business management, and leadership. Enhancing the app's UI, I focused on user experience and effective marketing strategies, refining my pitching skills through campus promotions. Process

In the Spring Quarter of 2024, I enrolled in the "Turn an Idea into a Company" class at UCLA. The course's objective was to transform an idea into a viable company within nine weeks. Noticing a gap in the availability of a centralized platform for students to discover clubs, organizations, and workshops on campus, I co-founded BruinBuzz, an iOS app designed to streamline access to this information. This report details the process of developing and launching BruinBuzz, highlighting the skills gained and challenges faced along the way.

UCLA lacked an everyday online platform where students could easily discover the whereabouts of various campus activities. The current solution, Bruinwalk, involved physical promoters who were often ignored by students rushing to classes. Recognizing this gap, I initiated the development of BruinBuzz, an app to centralize information about club events, workshops, and promotional activities, making it accessible at students' fingertips.

I assembled a team of four, including Chief Technology Officer Yahir Dimas, Marketing Manager Eric Chakhoyan, and Graphics Designer Chianie Chi. Despite having no prior experience in business management or programming in Swift, I embraced the challenge. My CTO and I dedicated countless late nights to learning Swift and developing the app. We brainstormed ideas to enhance and simplify the user interface, ensuring the app was user-friendly.

Throughout the development process, we faced numerous challenges. Learning Swift from scratch was demanding, but it was essential for creating a functional iOS app. We structured our work by setting deadlines and organizing meetings to track progress. Collaborating closely, we continuously improved the app's UI, focusing on optimizing performance and considering the user's perspective. Despite our efforts, we faced two rejections from Apple before our app was finally approved on May 21st, 2024.

Following the app's approval, we successfully launched BruinBuzz on the App Store. On May 30th, our team presented BruinBuzz during our class's "Demo Day" to a group of investors, including Michael Stoppleman, brother of Yelp's Co-Founder and CEO, Jeremy Stoppleman. This presentation was a significant milestone, providing us with valuable feedback and boosting our confidence.

Throughout this process, I acquired and honed several skills. I quickly adapted to programming in Swift, learned effective business management techniques, and embraced the responsibilities of being a CEO. Leading a team taught me the importance of collaboration and organization. I improved my problem-solving abilities by addressing challenges like app rejections and continuously enhancing the app. The marketing efforts helped me develop effective promotion strategies and refine my pitching skills. Presenting to investors further enhanced my confidence and public speaking abilities.

The development and launch of BruinBuzz were invaluable experiences that taught me essential skills in programming, business management, and leadership. The process of turning an idea into a functional app within nine weeks was both challenging and rewarding. I am proud of my team's accomplishments and look forward to continuing to improve and promote BruinBuzz. This experience has been the highlight of my time at UCLA, providing me with practical knowledge and a strong foundation for future endeavors.
